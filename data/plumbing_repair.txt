DOCUMENT: PLUMBING LEAKS AND REPAIR GUIDE
TOPIC: Fixing Sink Leaks and Replacing Washers

CRITICAL SAFETY PROTOCOLS:
1. ALWAYS locate and shut off the main water supply valve or the local shut-off valve under the sink before performing any work.
2. Place a bucket or shallow pan and rags directly beneath the plumbing joint or P-trap to catch any residual water.
3. If dealing with hot water lines, allow the pipes to cool completely before touching them to prevent burns.
4. Wear safety glasses to protect your eyes from debris, rust, or mineral deposits.

REQUIRED TOOLS:
- Adjustable wrench (Crescent wrench)
- Tongue-and-groove pliers (Channel locks)
- Teflon thread seal tape
- Bucket and dry rags
- Replacement rubber washers or O-rings
- Plumber's putty

PROCEDURE FOR FIXING A LEAKING SINK DRAIN / P-TRAP:
Step 1: Shut off the water supply by turning the silver knobs under the sink clockwise until they stop.
Step 2: Place a bucket under the P-trap (the J-shaped pipe underneath the sink).
Step 3: Unscrew the slip nuts connecting the P-trap to the sink drain and the wall pipe. Use tongue-and-groove pliers if the nuts are tight, but wrap the jaws in a rag to avoid scratching chrome pipes.
Step 4: Remove the P-trap joint and drain the remaining water into the bucket. Clean any sludge or debris out of the trap.
Step 5: Inspect the rubber washers inside the connections. If they are cracked, stiff, or misshapen, replace them with matching new washers.
Step 6: Reassemble the P-trap. Tighten the slip nuts by hand, then add a quarter-turn with the pliers. Do not over-tighten, especially on plastic pipes, or they will crack.
Step 7: Turn the water supply back on and run the faucet while inspecting all joints. If a slow drip remains, wrap the male threads of the pipe in Teflon tape (3-4 wraps clockwise) and re-tighten.

PROCEDURE FOR FIXING A DRIPPING FAUCET STEM:
Step 1: Shut off the water supply valves under the sink.
Step 2: Remove the decorative cap on the faucet handle, unscrew the handle screw, and pull the handle off.
Step 3: Use an adjustable wrench to loosen the brass packing nut on the cartridge stem and lift the stem out of the faucet body.
Step 4: Check the rubber washer at the base of the stem or the cartridge O-ring. If it is damaged or worn, replace it.
Step 5: Place a small amount of plumber's grease on the new washer/O-ring, reassemble the cartridge stem, tighten the packing nut, and reinstall the handle.
Step 6: Turn the water supply back on and check for drips.
